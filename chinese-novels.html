Wolf Totem by Jiang Rong
527 pages

1970s. Chen Zhen is among the Beijing university students who have come to Mongolia to be rusticated. As a sheepherder, he fears wolves. But when Chen learns from his adoptive father Bilgee why the Olonbulag revere wolves, Chen attempts to raise one. Translated from Chinese. Man Asian Literary Prize. 2008.

Soul mountain by Gao Xingjian
510 pages

A semi-autobiographical, philosophical novel by the Chinese Nobel laureate. The protagonist, in reprieve from death, wanders through the Chinese landscape in search of Lingshan (Soul Mountain), describing his interior and exterior quests. Some descriptions of sex and some strong language. Bestseller. 2000.

Balzac and the Little Chinese Seamstress by Dai Sijie
184 pages

Two privileged teenage boys are sent to a remote village for reeducation during China's Cultural Revolution. When they procure a supply of French books, they read novels to a pretty but uneducated seamstress, with surprising results. Some descriptions of sex and some strong language. Bestseller. 2001.
