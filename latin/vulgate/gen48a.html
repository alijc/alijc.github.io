AJC When this had been settled, it was reported to Joseph that his father was ill: and he took up his two sons, Manasses and Ephraim, and set out.

AJC And they said to the old man: Lo, thy son Joseph is coming to thee. And he drew strength and sat up in bed.

AJC And after he had come in he said to him: The almighty God appeared to me in Luza, in the land of Canaan: and blessed me,

AJC and said: I will increase thee and multiply thee, and will make thee into crowds of people: and I will give this land to thee, and to thy seed in everlasting possession after thee.

AJC Therefore thy two sons, who were born to thee in the land of Egypt before I came here to thee, will be my own: Ephraim and Manasses, just as Ruben and Simeon will be counted as mine.

AJC But the rest, that thou begets after them, will be thine, and they will be called by the name of their brothers in their inheritance.

AJC For me, when I was coming from Mesopotamia, Rachel died in the land of Canaan while on the journey. It was in the spring: and I was going to Ephrata, and I buried her beside the road to Ephrata, which is also called Bethlehem.

AJC But when he saw Joseph's sons he said to him: Who are these?

AJC He answered: They are my sons, that God gave to me in this place. Bring them to me, he said, that I might bless them.

AJC For Israel's eyes had clouded over, because of his extreme age, and he could not see clearly. And when they had been brought near, he kissed them warmly and embraced them,

AJC and said to his son: I am not cheated of thy sight: moreover God has shown thy seed to me.
