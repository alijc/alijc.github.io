18 AJC But Juda approached nearer and boldly said: Prithee my lord, let thy servant speak a word in thy ear, and be not angry with thy servant: for thou art, after Pharaoh

19 AJC my lord. Previously, thou asked thy servants: Have you a father or brother?

20 AJC and we replied to thee my lord: There is our elderly father, and a young boy, who was born in his old age: whose womb-brother has died: and his mother has him only, Father will truly love him tenderly.

21 AJC And thou said to thy servants: Bring him to me, and I will set my eyes upon him.

22 AJC We suggested to my lord: The boy cannot leave his father: for if he will have deserted him, he will die.

23 AJC And thou said to thy servants: Unless your youngest brother comes with you, you will not see my face again.

24 AJC Therefore, when we went up to thy servant our father, we told him all that my lord had said.

25 AJC And our father said: Return, and buy us a bit of wheat.

26 AJC And we said to him: We cannot go: if our youngest brother were to go down with us, we will depart at once: otherwise, if he is missing, we dare not see the man's face.

27 AJC To which he replied: You know that my wive bore two sons to me.

28 AJC One went out, and you said: a wild animal devoured him: and to this day he had not been seen.

29 AJC If you also carry off this one, and anything happens to him on the road, you will pull down my gray hair amid sorrow to hell.

30 AJC Therefore, if I reach thy servant our father, and the boy be lacking (since the former's spirit depends up the latter's)

31 AJC and he sees that he's not with us, he will die, and thy servants will pull down his gray hair amid sorrow to hell.

32 AJC I myself would be thy servant - whereby I guaranteed this in good faith, and I gave my word, saying: Unless I bring him back, I will be a party to offense to my father for all time.

33 AJC And so I will remain thy servant in place of the boy in attendence on my lord.

34 AJC For I cannot return to my father, lacking the boy: I will not stand witness to the calamities that oppress my father.
