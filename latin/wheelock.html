TR 3 AJC Proconsul Gnaeus Magnus sends greetings to General Cicero If you are well, it is good. I read your letter with pleasure; for I recognized your former strength still in the public welfare. The consuls, departing from Rome, came to the army that I kept in Apulia. I encourage you greatly to seize the chance and carry yourself to us, so that we might, through joint action, bring help and assistance to the unhappy republic. I advise you to leave Rome, to travel along the Appian Way, and to come as quick as you can to Brundisium. General Caesar sends greetings to General Cicero Although I am en route to Brundisium and am swiftly approaching it, having already sent my army on ahead, I nevertheless ought to write you and to give you proper thanks, even though I have often done this and seem to be doing it more often; you are so deserving. Especially, since I believe that I will quickly come to the city, I entreat you that I might see you there, so that I can make use of your advice, position, and power. Pardon the haste and brevity of my letter; the rest you will learn from Furnius.
